Personal Health

I have run many miles throughout my life, but never really got into time trials. When I lived in Korea, I would sometimes run for up to four hours at a time. In 2017 I moved to San Francisco and ran multiple 5k & 10k timed runs with no real goal in mind. I quickly found myself enjoying the friendly yet competitive nature of running in groups. Since then I have set my sites on running sub 6 minute miles in a 10k run. Halfway through the season, I clocked a 6:58 per mile on a 5k, but shortly after that I stepped on a nail while helping a friend do some yard work. More recently, I have been slowly gathering resources and rehabilitating myself in the hope of one day running in a marathon. Till then I have been sustaining my cardiovascular fitness through distance swimming and road biking.





Worm Farm

I started a worm farm after moving into my deceased grandparentsâ€™ house. There is a backyard with trees and plants that bear fruit that do not ripen due to the malnourished soil. I started a Red Wiggler Worm Farm with a population of 300 with the idea of growing a colony of 10,000+ worms. The main purpose of this is to harvest the nutrient-rich soil consisting of worm waste, which includes beneficial fungi and bacteria. This will help rehabilitate the garden back to what it once was.
